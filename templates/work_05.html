005 : Automatic Street Light System

Link:

Overview:

Developed a prototype for a smart street lighting system aimed at improving urban lighting efficiency and safety. The system utilized LEDs controlled by Light Dependent Resistor (LDR) sensors and object detection technology to adjust brightness based on real-time environmental conditions. Integrated XBee transmitters with Arduino Uno for real-time monitoring and fault notifications, and applied Python-based linear regression to analyze collected data for optimizing power consumption and operational costs.

Technology Used:

Python, IoT, Arduino, XBee, LDR Sensors, Object Detection Sensors, LEDs

Outcome:

Successfully created and demonstrated a working prototype that adapts street light brightness based on environmental conditions and object detection, illustrating potential improvements in urban lighting systems. Incorporated a fault detection system within the prototype to highlight maintenance needs, which can be applied to real-world systems to reduce downtime. Applied data analytics to the prototype’s performance data, identifying potential optimizations for power consumption and operational cost reductions. Showcased the potential for smart city infrastructure enhancements through the integration of sensors, wireless communication, and data analytics, laying the groundwork for future developments in urban lighting systems.

Project Team:

Medha Kumble, Niharika Pattabhi, Shreyas Tulpule, and Yashodeep Pawar
